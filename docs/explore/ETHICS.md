# ETHICS: Moral Philosophy and Value Systems

## Premise

Ethics asks: who decides? What is preserved? What is owed? The fitness function is an encoding of values — it determines what survives. When ZK proofs remove humans from the loop, whose values are encoded in the circuit?

---

## Frameworks

### Deontological Ethics (Kant)
- Duties and rules
- "You must" regardless of consequences
- Categorical imperative

**Synapse:** Governance expressions as duties. Invariants as categorical constraints.

### Consequentialism (Utilitarianism)
- Outcomes matter most
- Greatest good for greatest number
- Measurable

**Synapse:** Fitness function as consequentialist. f(Link) measures outcomes.

### Virtue Ethics (Aristotle)
- Character and habits
- What the good agent does
- Who you become

**Synapse:** The council's character. The fabric's "virtues" encoded in governance.

### Care Ethics (Gilligan, Noddings)
- Relationships and responsibilities
- Particular rather than universal
- Context matters

**Synapse:** Scoped governance. Particular communities have particular values.

### Contractarianism (Rawls)
- Fair agreements among rational agents
- Veil of ignorance
- Justice as fairness

**Synapse:** Genesis as founding contract. Governance as amendment procedure.

### Capabilities Approach (Sen, Nussbaum)
- What people can do and be
- Freedom as capability
- Minimum core

**Synapse:** What capabilities does a blob have? What can the fabric enable?

### Value Alignment (AI Safety)
- Ensuring AI pursues human values
- Corrigibility
- Intent specification

**Synapse:** Who specifies the fitness function? How is it audited?

---

## Key Tensions

### 1. Whose Fitness?

The fitness function encodes values. Who writes it?
- Single principal?
- Council?
- Market mechanism?
- Emergent from usage?

### 2. Accountability at Scale

When f_n = f_{n-1}(f_{n-1}) and no human reviewed f_n:
- Who is accountable for harm?
- How do you trace responsibility?
- What recourse exists?

### 3. The Encoding Problem

ZK proofs remove humans from the loop. But:
- Whose values are in the circuit?
- How is the circuit audited?
- What happens when values change?

### 4. Access and Power

Who can contribute? Who benefits?
- Technical barriers exclude
- Governance barriers gatekeep
- Economic barriers price out

### 5. The Commons Problem

The vault is a commons. Commons need governance to avoid tragedy. But:
- Who governs the governance?
- How are externalities priced?
- What happens to dissenting voices?

## Open Questions

1. **Who audits the fitness function?** The ZK circuit encodes values. Who reviews it?

2. **What is owed to deprecated blobs?** When a blob is superseded, what obligations exist?

3. **How do values evolve?** Can governance expressions change values, or are invariants immutable?

4. **What is the minimum ethical floor?** What must the fabric never do regardless of fitness?

5. **Who has standing?** What makes a stakeholder in the fabric's evolution?
