# SUBSTRATE.md — The Material Foundation

**Status:** Soft Definition (f_0-f_11 baseline)

---

## The Soft Definition

The **Substrate** is the **physical and informational medium** upon which the fabric exists. It is the irreducible "Ground" of the system.

In Synapse, the Substrate is the **Content-Addressable Vault** (the `blob_vault`). It is where the "Mass" of the system resides.

---

## The Role: Preservation

The Substrate has one primary job: **Invariant II (Inviolability)**. 
It ensures that once a sequence of bits is committed, it is preserved exactly as it was, accessible forever by its BLAKE3 hash.

---

## Relationship: Substrate vs. World

While often used interchangeably, there is a topological distinction:

1.  **The Substrate (The Physics):** The raw bits, the files on disk (or in memory), and the hashing algorithm. It is the "Soil."
2.  **The World (The Ecology):** The graph of **Nodes** and **Expressions** growing out of the soil. It is the "Mycelium."

You can have one **Substrate** that hosts multiple **Worlds** (Partitions/Tenants).

---

## Vantages

### [PHYSICIST] — Information Conservation
The Substrate is the medium that obeys the laws of information conservation. It is where entropy is recorded and where identity is forged.

### [BIOLOGIST] — The Growing Medium
The Substrate provides the nutrients (data) for the fruiting bodies (logical Isolates) to emerge. A healthy Substrate is one that is deduplicated and reachable.

### [MATHEMATICIAN] — The Set of Values
The Substrate is the set $S$ of all valid content-blobs. The **Arbiter** defines the functions that operate upon this set.

---

## Summary

The Substrate is the **"Where"** that has been replaced by the **"What."** In a traditional system, the substrate is the RAM/Disk address space. In Synapse, the substrate is the **Content-Addressable Identity Space**.
